You must read and accept all terms below before registering as a donor on Blood Connector. These terms protect you, patients, and the integrity of Ghana's blood supply.

Important legal notice

Blood Connector is a regulated platform operating under Ghanaian law. Blood is not a commodity and may not be sold under any circumstances. Any attempt to sell, buy, or broker blood through this platform is a criminal offence punishable by fine and/or imprisonment under the Criminal Offences Act 1960 (Act 29) and the National Blood Service regulations. Violations will be reported to the Ghana Police Service and relevant health authorities.

01 Eligibility & voluntary participation

Blood donation on this platform is strictly voluntary. No person may be coerced, pressured, incentivised financially, or otherwise compelled to donate blood. By registering, you confirm that your participation is entirely of your own free will.

To be eligible to register as a donor you must:

  • Be at least 17 years of age at the time of registration
  • Be a resident of Ghana with a valid Ghana Card issued by the National Identification Authority (NIA)
  • Weigh a minimum of 50kg (110 lbs)
  • Not have donated blood in the past 56 days (8 weeks)
  • Be in good general health and not currently on antibiotics or blood-thinning medication
  • Not have chronic illnesses or conditions that medically prohibit donation
  • Not have travelled to a malaria-endemic area in the past 12 months without medical clearance

02 Prohibition on sale of blood

Blood must not be bought or sold. It is a criminal offence under Ghanaian law to receive payment — whether in cash, goods, services, or any other form of compensation — in exchange for donating blood.

Blood Connector facilitates connections between willing voluntary donors and accredited medical facilities. It does not and will never facilitate commercial blood transactions. Any donor found to be soliciting, accepting, or negotiating payment for blood will be:

  • Permanently removed from the platform
  • Reported to the National Blood Service of Ghana and the Ghana Police Service
  • Subject to prosecution under the Criminal Offences Act 1960 (Act 29)

03 Identity verification & data protection

You consent to Blood Connector verifying your identity using the National Identification Authority (NIA) API. Your Ghana Card number, full legal name, date of birth, and photograph will be cross-referenced with NIA records. Submitting false or fraudulent identity documents is a criminal offence.

Your personal data including name, phone number, blood type, region, and profile photo will be stored securely and used solely for the purpose of donor matching. By registering you consent to:

  • Your profile being visible to registered, verified hospitals and clinics on Blood Connector
  • Your phone number being shared with hospital users who contact you
  • Your approximate location being used for proximity-based matching
  • Your donation history being recorded and stored on our secure platform

Your data will not be sold or shared with third parties outside the platform for commercial purposes.

04 Medical responsibility & liability

Blood Connector is a connection platform only. We do not employ medical staff, supervise blood collection, or take responsibility for clinical outcomes. All actual blood donation and transfusion procedures are performed solely by licensed medical professionals at accredited facilities.

You are responsible for ensuring your own medical fitness before donating. If you have any doubts about your health status, you must consult a doctor before agreeing to donate. Blood Connector bears no liability for any adverse health outcomes arising from donation.

05 Platform conduct & violations

Donors are expected to conduct themselves professionally and ethically at all times. The following actions will result in immediate removal from the platform and may result in legal action:

  • Providing false medical or identity information
  • Soliciting or accepting payment for blood donation
  • Harassing, threatening, or intimidating hospital staff or other users
  • Misrepresenting your blood type or donation history
  • Confirming a donation that did not take place
  • Using the platform for any purpose other than voluntary blood donation

Blood Connector reserves the right to report violations to the Ghana Police Service, the National Blood Service, and the Health Facilities Regulation Authority.
