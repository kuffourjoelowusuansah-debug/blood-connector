All hospitals, clinics, and medical practitioners must read and accept these terms before registering on Blood Connector. These terms govern your access to the donor network.

Critical legal notice — medical facilities

Only accredited medical facilities licensed by the Health Facilities Regulation Authority (HeFRA) may register on Blood Connector. Blood must not be purchased, sold, or commercially traded under any circumstances. Any facility found soliciting or compensating donors financially will be reported to HeFRA, the National Blood Service, and the Ghana Police Service and may face licence revocation, fines, and criminal prosecution.

01 Facility eligibility & accreditation

To register on Blood Connector your facility must meet all of the following requirements:

  • Be a licensed medical facility registered with the Health Facilities Regulation Authority (HeFRA)
  • Possess a valid and current facility operating licence
  • Have qualified medical staff authorised to conduct blood transfusions
  • Be registered with the Ghana Health Service or operate under a recognised private medical licence
  • Have a designated contact person (doctor or administrator) accountable for platform use

You are required to upload supporting documentation during registration including your HeFRA licence, facility photos, and any relevant medical accreditation. Blood Connector reserves the right to reject or suspend facilities that cannot provide adequate documentation.

HeFRA Act 1996 (Act 525)Ghana Health Service Act 1996 (Act 525)

02 Prohibition on blood trafficking & payment

Under no circumstances may your facility offer, pay, or arrange any form of compensation to a donor for blood. This includes cash, gifts, services, favourable treatment, or any other benefit that could constitute commercial inducement to donate.

Facilities in violation of this provision will face:

  • Immediate and permanent suspension from Blood Connector
  • Formal report to the Health Facilities Regulation Authority (HeFRA) for licence review
  • Referral to the Ghana Police Service for criminal investigation
  • Potential prosecution under the Criminal Offences Act 1960 (Act 29)

Hospitals may, however, offer non-financial privileges such as free health screenings, priority consultations, or other health-related benefits to donors as a gesture of goodwill — provided these are listed transparently on the platform and do not constitute commercial inducement.

Criminal Offences Act 1960 (Act 29)National Blood Service Act

03 Donor contact & ethical conduct

Donor contact information accessed through Blood Connector may only be used for the purpose of requesting voluntary blood donation for a patient in need. Any other use of donor contact details — including marketing, solicitation, or non-medical communication — is a serious breach of these terms.

  • Donor phone numbers must not be stored or used outside of this platform's intended purpose
  • Harassment or intimidation of donors will result in immediate account suspension
  • Donation must never be presented as mandatory or coerced in any way
  • Facilities must inform donors clearly that participation is voluntary before and during all communications

Data Protection Act 2012 (Act 843)Electronic Communications Act 2008 (Act 775)

04 Accurate donation confirmation

Your facility is responsible for accurately confirming blood donations through the platform. A donation may only be marked as successful when it has genuinely occurred under proper medical supervision at your facility.

Falsely confirming a donation that did not take place — or failing to confirm one that did — constitutes platform abuse and may amount to fraud. Specifically:

  • Only mark donations as complete when blood was actually collected
  • Do not confirm donations to inflate a donor's record
  • Disputed confirmations will be reviewed by Blood Connector administrators
  • Repeated false confirmations will result in account suspension and investigation

Criminal Offences Act 1960 (Act 29) — Fraud provisions

05 Subscription, payments & access

Access to the Blood Connector hospital portal requires a monthly subscription of GHS 300, payable via Paystack. Subscription fees are used to maintain and grow the platform and support the verified donor network.

  • Subscriptions auto-renew monthly unless cancelled
  • Access will be suspended if payment fails for more than 7 days
  • A free trial may be offered at the discretion of Blood Connector administration
  • Refunds are not provided for partial months of access
  • Blood Connector reserves the right to change subscription pricing with 30 days' notice

Consumer Protection Act 2020 (Act 1060)

06 Data protection & documentation

By registering, your facility authorises Blood Connector to store and display your facility name, location, contact details, offered donor privileges, and verified accreditation status on the platform.

Documentation uploaded during registration including facility photos, licences, and accreditation certificates will be reviewed by Blood Connector administrators and stored securely. This information may be used to verify your facility's legitimacy to donors and the public.

All data handling is governed by the Data Protection Act 2012 (Act 843). Patient data submitted in blood request forms is treated as confidential medical information and is not publicly disclosed.
